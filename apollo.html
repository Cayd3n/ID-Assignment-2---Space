Apollo Missions Timeline

Apollo 1 - Feburary 21
"Apollo's Broken Arrow"

The first mission of the Apollo series, the maiden flight of the Apollo program ended in tragedy as flames claimed the control panel and three astronauts lost their lives. No missions were ever named Apollo 2 or 3.

1967

Apollo 7 - October 11
"Dancing With The Stars"

The next crewed Apollo mission, the Apollo 7 concluded successfully with the first american TV broadcast from space.

1968

Apollo 8 - December 21
"To The Moon And Back."

The third crewed Apollo mission, the Apollo 8 had successfully completed its task of circling the moon and returning back safe

1968

Apollo 9 - March 3
"Spider's First Mission"

This fourth crewed Apollo mission tested out the capabilities of the first crewed lunar module, two broadcasts were made in the 10 days the mission spanned. Before the crew made a safe re-entry to earth

1969

Apollo 10 - May 18
"Looking Before One Leaps"

The fifth crewed Apollo mission served as a dry run for the actual moon landing, the Apollo 10 entered the Moon's orbit for surveying work before returning back to Earth.

1969

Apollo 11 - July 16
"The Eagle Has Landed"

The sixth and most famous of Apollo missions, the Apollo 11 is the first time in human history where man set foot on the moon, with the planting of the American flag, the US swore victory in the Space Race.

1969
